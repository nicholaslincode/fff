History of FFF:

In the frosty depths of winter, in the year 2023, a daring band of misfits gathered to undertake a feat many deemed impossible: the alchemical fermentation of tempeh. This endeavor was to be undertaken within the hallowed halls of a scholarly institution. Disregarding the strictures of aseptic practice, the room was strewn with remnants of revelry — chips and biscuits scattered upon the working table, a boardgame was underway, and a poopy-diapered baby was mere feet away but a stone's throw from the experimental setup. Undeterred by these distractions, the intrepid collective pressed on, their labor shrouded in secrecy as they toiled on a fateful Friday at the hour of five past noon. Miraculously, by Monday's break of dawn, the soy beans had undergone its mystic transformation, and by the midweek, the tempeh was partaken of by a chosen few, none of whom suffered any ill effects. Some whispered in awe that their concoction surpassed even the grandest expectations. Such success, defying all conventional wisdom, begged a singular question: could it be that the poopy-diapered baby, so innocently nestled nearby, was not merely mortal flesh, but none other than the second coming of the Messiah? Was it this holy presence that blessed the tempeh with abundant success? In the wake of this triumph, the concept of FFF was born — a celebration of fermentation and the microbial forces that guide it. And so, each month, as the month wanes, this fellowship gathers to honor this mystical craft, their hearts brimming with gratitude for all things fermented.
